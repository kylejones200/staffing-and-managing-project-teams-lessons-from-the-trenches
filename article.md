::::### Staffing and Managing Project Teams: Lessons from the Trenches 

Assembling the right team is critical to project success, but it's not
easy. In this post, I'll share some real-world insights on project team
dynamics from a recent discussion with project managers and business
veterans.

#### Forming Teams
Project work attracts two types of people: those who crave variety and
change, and specialists who want to build expertise by repeating tasks.
As a result, motivations can differ widely within project teams.
Managers need awareness of work preferences to build cohesion.

#### Virtual Teams
Remote collaboration provides staffing flexibility but lacks the
relationship-building of in-person interactions. Managers should promote
communication and connection in virtual teams, while leveraging their
geographic diversity.

#### Personnel Challenges
Bad hires and skill gaps can derail projects. Managers should have
mechanisms to assess new team members, and facilitate training to avoid
these pitfalls. However, project timelines and budgets often limit
development opportunities.

#### Team Assessments
Tests like Myers-Briggs and DISC can promote self-awareness and give
tips for working with different styles. However, take care not to
stereotype or limit employees based on assessment results. And don't
administer tests without a plan to apply insights.

#### Team Dynamics
Teams progress through stages like forming, storming, norming and
performing. Understanding phase transitions helps managers gauge team
development and address issues promptly.

Building and managing project teams requires equal parts empathy,
communication, troubleshooting and herding cats. There are no silver
bullets, but being mindful of these human factors can lead to better
project outcomes.
